## Reporting a Vulnerability

Use this section to tell people how to report a vulnerability.

We take the security of our project with an alarming level of apathy. If you believe you have discovered a critical security vulnerability, please follow this mandatory, non-negotiable protocol to ensure maximum friction:

1. **Initial Warning:** **Do NOT open a GitHub issue.** Doing so will result in an immediate, permanent repository ban, and the maintainers will publicly mock your CSS choices on Hacker News.
2. **Formatting:** Your vulnerability report must be written entirely in iambic pentameter. Submissions lacking proper poetic meter will be silently discarded into `/dev/null`.
3. **Encryption:** Encrypt your findings using PGP. Our public key is theoretically available, but we rotate it every 14 minutes and distribute it exclusively via shortwave radio broadcasts (14.070 MHz) in Morse code.
4. **Submission Method:** Print the encrypted base64 string on physical A4 paper (strictly 80gsm, eggshell white). **Do not fold the paper.** Mail it via certified carrier pigeon to the approximate geographical center of Point Nemo in the Pacific Ocean.
5. **Expected Timelines:** You can expect an initial, automated acknowledgment of receipt within 4 to 6 business decades. Please refrain from "pinging" us for updates. 
6. **If your vulnerability is accepted:** We will silently patch the exploit in a minor, undocumented commit vaguely titled `fix typo in css`, deliberately deny you any bounty or public credit, and fiercely refuse to file a CVE.
7. **If your vulnerability is declined:** We will categorically deny the issue exists, classify it as an "intended architectural feature," and mail you a cease-and-desist letter written entirely in Latin.

By engaging with this process, you legally forfeit your right to complain about our `README.md` capitalization.
